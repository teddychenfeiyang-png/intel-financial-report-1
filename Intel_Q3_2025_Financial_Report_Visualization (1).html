⚙️Operational Transformation under CEO Lip-Bu Tan

Workforce Optimisation

Reduced from 124,100 to 88,400 employees (-28.8%). Streamlined organisation, eliminated inefficiencies, retained top talent. Target: 75,000 by year-end.

Operating Expense Control

OpEx down 59.5% from $11.1B to $4.5B. Demonstrates disciplined cost management whilst maintaining R&D investments in critical areas.

CapEx Discipline

2025 gross CapEx expected at ~$18B (down from initial guidance). Net CapEx $8-11B. Cancelled Germany and Poland foundry projects; slowed Ohio construction.

Portfolio Streamlining

Exited Altera (51% sold), partial Mobileye monetisation. Focus shifted to core x86 platforms, AI accelerators, and foundry services.

Transformation Strategy

  • Organisational Restructuring: Reduced management layers to accelerate decision-making
  • Return-to-Office: Implemented 4-day office mandate to boost collaboration and productivity
  • Foundry Discipline: Growth capacity only with committed volume; deploy CapEx lockstep with tangible milestones
  • Cost Target: Operating expenses targeted at $17B (2025) and $16B (2026)

🏭Intel Foundry: The Critical Pivot

Current Status: Internal Only

$4.2B Q3 revenue, 100% from Intel. No external customer revenue yet. This remains the biggest question mark for long-term viability.

18A Node Progress

Fab 52 in Arizona fully operational. Panther Lake scheduled for market launch in 2025. Yields progressing predictably. Foundation for next 3 generations.

Advanced Packaging

EMIB and EMIB-T differentiation. Strong progress in packaging services creating competitive advantage. Growing customer interest.

External Customer Pipeline

Active engagement, no announced wins. Microsoft rumoured for AI chip manufacturing. U.S. Government secure enclave work committed.

14A Next Generation

Designed as foundry-first node. Ground-up approach to meet customer requirements. Addresses broader market segments beyond internal needs.

Capital Requirements

$100B total investment needed. Long-term commitment requiring sustained customer wins and government support to justify.

Foundry Strategy & Outlook

  • Competitive Positioning: U.S.-based leading-edge manufacturing unique advantage vs TSMC geographic concentration
  • AI Infrastructure Demand: Unprecedented capacity needs for AI creating opportunities for multiple suppliers
  • Strategic Partnerships: NVIDIA, Microsoft, and U.S. Government engagement signals potential customer traction
  • Risk Factor: Foundry business still unproven; zero external revenue raises execution concerns

⚠️Key Risks & Challenges

Foundry Customer Risk

Zero external customer revenue. Requires major customer wins (Microsoft, Apple, others) to validate $100B investment thesis.

AI Market Share Deficit

Trailing NVIDIA (90%+ AI chip share) and AMD. Gaudi 3 AI accelerator unproven at scale. Difficult to regain lost ground.

Margin Recovery Uncertainty

5% operating margin still far below historic 55% levels. Path to sustainable high margins unclear given competitive dynamics.

Government Funding Accounting

$5.7B government stake accounting treatment pending SEC review. Results may be revised, creating uncertainty.

Execution Risk

18A node critical for turnaround. Any delays or yield issues would severely damage credibility with potential foundry customers.

Competitive Pressure

AMD gaining x86 server share, NVIDIA dominating AI, TSMC 30-year manufacturing lead. Fighting on multiple fronts.
